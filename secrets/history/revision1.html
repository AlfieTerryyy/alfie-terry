What were the main points of the Treaty of Versailles?
The main points were: War Guilt Clause (Article 231), Reparations, Disarmament, Territorial Losses, and the establishment of the League of Nations.
What did the Treaty of Versailles force Germany to do?
Germany had to accept full responsibility for the war, pay reparations, give up territories, disarm, and lose all overseas colonies.
When was the Treaty of Versailles signed?
The treaty was signed on June 28, 1919.
How did the Treaty of Versailles lead to WWII?
The harsh reparations and territorial losses created economic hardship and resentment in Germany, contributing to the rise of nationalism and Adolf Hitler's regime.
Article 160
The German army was limited to 100,000 troops with no conscription allowed.
Article 181
The German navy was restricted to six battleships, with no submarines permitted.
Article 198
Germany was forbidden from having an air force, including military aircraft and airships.
Article 231 (War Guilt Clause)
Germany accepted responsibility for causing the war and had to pay reparations.
Article 119
Germany lost all of its overseas colonies, assigned as mandates to the League of Nations.
Article 42
The Rhineland was to be demilitarized, preventing German troops from being stationed there.
Article 80
Germany was forbidden from unifying with Austria, preventing the formation of a stronger state.
Article 87
Germany ceded territories such as Alsace-Lorraine to France and parts of Prussia to Poland.
